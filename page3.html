Tertiary Treatment

Tertiary treatment, also referred to as advanced wastewater treatment, is the final polishing stage in the wastewater treatment process. It follows primary and secondary treatment, aiming to remove even more pollutants and enhance the overall quality of the treated wastewater.

Here's what tertiary treatment targets:

1.Nutrients:Nitrogen and phosphorus, which can cause harmful algal blooms if present in excessive amounts in receiving water bodies.

2.Pathogens:Bacteria, viruses, and parasites that can pose health risks if the treated water is reused.

3.Emerging contaminants:These include pharmaceuticals, personal care products, and industrial chemicals that may not be effectively removed in earlier treatment stages.


Why is tertiary treatment necessary?

While primary and secondary treatment can significantly clean wastewater, tertiary treatment becomes crucial when the treated water needs to meet stricter standards for reuse applications. Examples include:

-Irrigation for agriculture or landscaping

-Industrial processes

-Groundwater recharge

-Indirect potable reuse (after further advanced treatment)


Tertiary treatment methods:

There are various techniques employed in tertiary treatment, and the specific choice depends on the desired level of treatment and the intended use of the treated water. Here are some common methods:

1.Filtration:This removes any remaining suspended solids and microorganisms through processes like sand filtration or membrane filtration.

2.Disinfection:Techniques like ultraviolet (UV) irradiation or chlorine disinfection eliminate harmful pathogens

3.Nutrient removal:Specialized biological or chemical processes can further reduce nitrogen and phosphorus levels.


Benefits of tertiary treatment:

-Water conservation:Enables recycling and reuse of treated wastewater, reducing pressure on freshwater resources.

-Environmental protection:Minimizes pollution in rivers, lakes, and other receiving water bodies

-Sustainable water management:Contributes to a more sustainable water cycle by creating a reliable source of high-quality treated water.

Overall, tertiary treatment plays a vital role in ensuring the safety and quality of treated wastewater, particularly when it's destined for reuse applications.
